pho

Description

Pho is a beloved Vietnamese noodle soup that features rice noodles swimming in a flavorful broth. Traditional pho recipes call for slowly simmering beef bones, spices like star anise and cinnamon, and aromatic herbs like ginger and onion to create a rich, complex broth. The broth is then ladled over rice noodles and topped with thinly sliced beef, herbs like cilantro and Thai basil, and additional garnishes like bean sprouts, lime wedges, and chili sauce. Pho recipes can vary by region, with some versions using chicken broth instead of beef. But the core elements - the fragrant broth, chewy rice noodles, and fresh herbs and vegetables - remain the hallmarks of this beloved Vietnamese comfort food.

Ingredients

broth

  • Beef bones
  • Onion, sliced
  • Ginger, sliced
  • Star anise
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Fennel seeds
  • Whole cloves
  • Coriander seeds
  • Fish sauce
  • Salt

soup

  • Rice noodles
  • Bean sprouts
  • Cilantro
  • Lime wedges
  • Thinly sliced beef

Steps

  1. Simmer beef bones, onion, ginger, and spices (star anise, cinnamon, cloves, fennel, coriander) for 3-4 hours
  2. Strain broth and season with fish sauce and salt
  3. Soak rice noodles until pliable
  4. Thinly slice beef
  5. Prepare garnishes: bean sprouts, Thai basil, cilantro, lime, chili sauce
  6. Bring broth to a simmer
  7. Place noodles in bowl, ladle hot broth over top
  8. Top with sliced beef, allowing it to cook in the broth
  9. Garnish with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime, and chili sauce as desired
